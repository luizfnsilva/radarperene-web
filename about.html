What is Radar Perene

Radar Perene is an editorial publication that reads Brazil’s market regime. It does not predict prices or offer opinions of the moment: it describes the present state of the market and records what has tended to follow similar moments. Regime first; the decision next — and that remains yours.

Who it’s for

For the investor, the analyst, the manager — and for any reader who wants to follow Brazil’s market without noise. Not tomorrow’s forecast, but the precedent: where we stand and what has followed moments like this one.

How we read Brazil

The Radar reads the country through five lenses, each covering a force that moves the market:

  • Wealth — succession, inheritance, holdings and the tax burden on wealth.
  • Electoral — the political calendar and the mood that precedes the vote.
  • Macro / Rates — interest rates, inflation, FX and the government’s accounts.
  • Institutional — the laws and decisions being written right now.
  • Real estate — property, REITs and the credit that moves them.

There is also the Vértice lens, the house laboratory: it crosses market, FX, crypto and mood to raise hypotheses about where capital is moving — always a hypothesis, never a forecast.

What we show

The regime reading for Brazil and the intermarket — how sectors and classes move against each other —, the house indices that summarize risk and mood, and the historical analogs from the past: “today recalls such an episode, which ended this way”. It is the day’s editorial read: descriptive, with the counter-evidence in plain sight.

Where the reading comes from

We read public sources — the market, the Central Bank, the Treasury, the courts — and translate them into a regime reading, with the historical precedent at hand, over an archive that begins in 2000. We show what happened and what tended to follow; never a forecast, and always with the misses in plain sight.

Is it investment advice?

No. Radar Perene is editorial, descriptive content from public sources. It describes the regime and context — it never recommends buying or selling, nor predicts price.

FAQ

What is Radar Perene?
An editorial publication that reads Brazil's market regime — macro, rates, country risk, intermarket and FX — across five lenses, plus the Vértice experiment. Descriptive, on public data.
What is a "market regime"?
The prevailing state of the market over a window — defensive, neutral or pro-risk. The first thing the Radar reads, before any detail.
How is Brazil's country risk today?
Today's reading is live at radarperene.com — Brazil and global regime, the intermarket and the proprietary indices. Descriptive, never a forecast.
What are the 5 lenses?
Wealth, Electoral, Macro/Rates, Institutional and Real estate — five domains of Brazil's regime. The Vértice lens is the cross-asset experiment.
Does Radar show quotes and tickers?
Radar Perene is an editorial reading of Brazil's market — regime, intermarket and the proprietary indices (Perene Risk, Ânima, ILI). The focus is macro and indices, not per-asset quotes: we are an editorial house, not a market terminal.
What is intermarket analysis?
Watching how sectors and classes move against each other (commodities, REITs, financials vs the IBOV; Vale vs gold) to read capital rotation.

No. It's educational and descriptive; it never recommends buying/selling nor predicts price.
