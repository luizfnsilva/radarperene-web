Who it's for

Investors, analysts, managers — and serious newcomers — who want to understand Brazil's market regime without noise and without guesswork, at the depth they choose: from a one-line read to the data behind it.

Two brains, five lenses

The Radar (conservative brain) reads Brazil's regime across five lenses:

  • Wealth — succession, estate tax, holdings and fiscal pressure on wealth.
  • Electoral — electoral courts, eligibility, campaign finance and the felt economy (a vote predictor).
  • Macro / Rates — ex-ante real rate, Selic, inflation, fiscal stress, debt and the curve.
  • Institutional — laws, taxes and decisions in the making that touch wealth and succession.
  • Real estate — REITs, real-estate credit, registries and the REITs–CDI spread.

The Vértice (experimental brain) adds the cross-asset layer: 6 thermometers (AI bubble, crypto liquidity, debt stress, commodities, FX, global and domestic risk…), market breadth, geographic risk-on/off, lead-lag relationships with statistical significance (FDR) and historical analog studies — "this configuration occurred N times; here's what tended to follow."

What it shows

Beyond the regime reading and BR intermarket (commodities, REITs, financials and utilities versus the IBOV), Radar shows a taste of tickers per lens: Brazilian stocks (price and sector), Treasury bonds (inflation-linked, fixed, Selic), REITs (dividend yield) and cryptocurrencies (price). The paid app goes deep into hundreds of tickers, free cross-analysis between them, and projection from the past.

How it works (high level)

A proprietary engine processes public data — markets, the Central Bank, the Treasury and regulatory/judicial activity (15 courts) — and produces a regime reading: 0–100 thermometers, divergences between signals, and analog studies with historical hit-rates, over 50+ years of series. Everything deterministic and descriptive: it shows what happened and what tended to follow, with the counter-evidence in plain sight, never as a forecast.

Is it investment advice?

No. Radar Perene is educational and informational content from public sources. It describes the regime and context — it never recommends buying or selling, nor predicts price.
