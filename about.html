Radar Perene: Brazil's regime, read as data.

An in-house intelligence instrument that reads Brazil's regulatory, judicial, macroeconomic and market environment — and shows, descriptively, what is happening today. Not opinion; a regime reading, from beginner to advanced.

Who it's for

Investors, analysts, managers and the curious who want to understand Brazil's market regime without noise and without guesswork — at the depth they choose, from a one-line read to advanced detail.

What it reads

Five conservative lenses (Radar): regulatory; rates & macro (real rate, term premium, fiscal stress, real curve slope); BR intermarket (commodities, REITs, financials, utilities vs the IBOV); legal (judicial activity quantified by area); and wealth.

The Vértice lens (experimental, premium): 9 cross-asset thermometers (AI bubble, crypto liquidity, sovereign debt stress, commodities, FX, global and domestic risk…), market breadth, geographic risk-on/off, and historical analog studies — "this configuration occurred N times; here's what tended to follow."

How it works (high level)

A proprietary engine processes public data and produces a regime reading: 0–100 thermometers, divergences between signals, and analog studies with historical hit-rates. Everything deterministic and descriptive — it shows what happened and what tended to follow, always with the counter-evidence in plain sight, never as a forecast.

Is it investment advice?

No. Radar Perene is educational and informational content from public sources. It describes the regime and context — it never recommends buying or selling, nor predicts price.
